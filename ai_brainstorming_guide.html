🧠 Brainstorm & Refine Research Questions with AI

Learn how AI tools can help you find, narrow, and shape your research topic.

✅ 1. Brainstorm Broad Topics

You can start with a general idea and ask AI to help break it down into potential research areas.

Example prompt: 
"I'm interested in climate change. What are some academic research angles I could explore?"

💡 AI might respond with ideas like:

  • The impact of climate change on coastal communities
  • Youth climate activism in different countries
  • Misinformation about climate science on social media

✅ 2. Narrow a Topic to a Manageable Focus

If your topic feels too broad, AI can help you filter it down based on scope, audience, method, or timeframe.

Prompt: 
"Help me narrow down a research question about social media and mental health that would work for a 10-page paper."

💡 AI might return:

  • How does TikTok usage affect sleep patterns in college students?
  • What role does social media play in shaping body image among teenagers?

✅ 3. Explore Unfamiliar Angles

AI can help you see your topic through different lenses: sociological, ethical, technological, or historical.

Prompt: 
"What are some ethical issues I could explore related to AI in education?"

💡 Results might include:

  • AI bias in grading and feedback tools
  • Data privacy in student AI usage
  • Teacher dependence on AI-generated content

✅ 4. Turn a Topic into a Research Question

Once you’ve picked a topic, you can ask AI to help you phrase it as an effective research question.

Prompt: 
"Turn this into a research question: the rise of remote work after COVID-19."

💡 Example results:

  • What are the long-term effects of remote work on productivity in the tech industry?
  • How has remote work reshaped urban migration trends post-COVID?

✅ 5. Compare and Refine Multiple Versions

You can even ask the AI to compare or rank options for originality, focus, or clarity.

Prompt: 
"Here are 3 versions of my research question. Which one is the strongest and why?"

✅ 6. Connect Class Readings to Topics

Use AI to turn books or course themes into research ideas.

Prompt: 
"Based on this summary of *Just Mercy*, what are some research questions I could explore related to justice and incarceration?"

💡 Tip: Paste in a short passage if you're using a specific reading.

✅ 7. Try These Prompts on Your Own

  • “Give me 5 research questions about [topic].”
  • “What are the emerging trends in [field]?”
  • “How can I turn this broad idea — [your idea] — into something more specific?”
  • “What’s a unique angle on [topic] that hasn’t been overdone?”

🧰 Tools to Use for This

  • ChatGPT (GPT-3.5 or GPT-4) — for creative phrasing and outlining
  • Claude — for a more conversational and exploratory tone
  • Perplexity.ai — for blending brainstorming with cited source discovery

⚠️ Academic Integrity and Citation Help

AI tools are just one part of your research journey. You still need to:

  • Evaluate sources
  • Verify facts
  • Avoid plagiarism
  • Cite your sources properly

Use tools like Zotero, Purdue OWL, or citation generators (e.g., MLA, APA, Chicago).
When in doubt, ask your professor or a librarian at Legacy Library.
